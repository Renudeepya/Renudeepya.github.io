About Me

My work bridges rigorous engineering, statistical modeling, and storytelling: leading enterprise data-governance initiatives to 95% lineage coverage with zero audit findings, automating quality controls that cut manual effort by 40%, and translating complex datasets into compliant, real-time business decisions.

I hold a Dual Degree in Sports Technology & Digital Transformation from Escuela Universitaria Real Madrid – Universidad Europea (with intensive coursework in Big Data & AI for Sports Management) and a Master’s in Information Systems (Business Analytics) from Stevens Institute of Technology. My research curiosity ignited with an undergraduate implementation of Low-Density Parity-Check codes in Python and MATLAB and continues through ongoing projects in causal inference, clustering, and predictive analytics.

Beyond data, I paint watercolor cityscapes and abstract emotions, sketch daily moments, and write poetry about memory, migration, and urban quietude. These practices sharpen the same instincts I bring to analytics — seeing hidden patterns, distilling complexity, and communicating insight with clarity and humanity.

My current work and independent research focus on trustworthy, scalable AI systems for high-stakes domains: financial risk modeling, sports performance analytics, and sustainable decision-making.
